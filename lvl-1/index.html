This is a paragraph within a div. A div "divides content" and it's the basic building block of layouts. Not really descriptive right? You might note that I've already described certain things that seem to act the same but aren't? In this game, that's called semantics, and it matters. Semantics matter because not everyone is using your typical browser to look at your website. There's blind people using screen-readers, and also search engines and even other websites that want to know your content in a way that MAKES SENSE. A <div> tag doesn't mean anything but "this is a block of content", it is not semantic. It's a block element, and it contains other stuff, or divides its content from other content.

Note I'm still using the paragraph tag. That's important, for semantics AND for styling stuff (when we get to it). There's literally NEVER a time you'd want to just put text in something other than an element meant for it.

And finally, tables. These often are misused to make layouts. Don't do that and you won't grow to hate them. Use these for displaying data that ought to be in a table and it'll make sense a lot easier.

Tables may consist of a header, a body, and a footer. Each of those contains rows and columns, and they're all sort of interlinked.

Head A1 Head B1
Cell A2 Cell B2
Cell A3 Cell B3

But tables gotta be a little flexible. Here, the body's first row cell spans two columns, so there's only one cell in that row:

Head A1 Head B1
Cell A2/B2
Cell A3 Cell B3

And we can use rowspan to do the same thing vertically:

Cell B3
